# The MCC Smart-Filtering Protocol

This charter defines the Merchant Category Code (MCC) restrictions for the network's Biometric NFC Cards. By mathematically locking funds to specific retail categories, we eliminate the need for manual receipt auditing, enforce the 90% Stewardship Standard, and guarantee to donors that the "Digital Bread" is used strictly for survival.

## 1. The "Survival Core" (Approved MCCs)
The Guardian AI automatically authorizes transactions at the following merchant categories to ensure funds are seamlessly available for food, health, and transit:
* **5411 (Grocery Stores & Supermarkets):** Approved for food and hygiene items.
* **5912 (Drug Stores & Pharmacies):** Approved for medical supplies and prescriptions.
* **4111 (Local Commuter Transportation):** Approved for bus passes and local transit.
* **5651 (Family Clothing Stores):** Approved for seasonal clothing replacement.
* **5310 (Discount Stores):** Approved for general survival hardware.

## 2. The Auto-Decline Ledger (Restricted MCCs)
To maintain donor trust and civic integrity, the following categories are hard-coded for immediate decline. Any attempt to use the card at these locations triggers a zero-balance read and logs a "Friction Alert" for the local Fork's Human Connections lead:
* **5921 (Package Stores / Liquor):** Automatically Declined.
* **5813 (Bars, Taverns, Nightclubs):** Automatically Declined.
* **7995 (Betting, Casino Gaming):** Automatically Declined.

## 3. Hardware Integration & Durability
Because the MCC smart-filtering logic is carried onto the street by the recipients, the physical distribution devices must survive extreme conditions. It is mandatory that all biometric cards provided to the unhoused are completely **EMP-hardened** and **camp-proof** to withstand vandalism, weather, and electrical interference. Furthermore, the cards are manufactured using alternative, highly durable materials with absolutely no Teflon or plastics in the design.
