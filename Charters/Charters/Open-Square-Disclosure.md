# The Open Square Disclosure Protocol

In the Relay Pulse network, we recognize that true civic resilience requires absolute transparency, especially when mistakes are made. We do not bury our failures in private.

## 1. The Public Interest Reset
If a Fork makes a poor investment—such as a failed software node or an ineffective local pilot program—the record is not quietly deleted. It undergoes a **Public Disclosure**.
* **Impotentizing the Error:** By publishing the failed code or budget on the Public Ledger, we render that mistake powerless to harm other Forks.
* **The Reset:** We admit the operational "debt," clear it through total transparency, and invite the community to audit the recovery process.

## 2. R&D Pivot via Disclosure
Failures are the primary fuel for the Jerusalem R&D Hub. When a project fails, the ashes of that failure are analyzed by the Guardian AI to build stronger logic for the next iteration. 

## 3. The Contrast to Traditional Systems
Traditional bureaucracies often hide closed programs and canceled contracts to protect institutional reputation. The Open Square Protocol guarantees that the public always has access to the raw data of both our triumphs and our missteps, ensuring the community trust remains unbroken.
